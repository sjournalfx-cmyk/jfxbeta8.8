## 5 Reply Templates (use these daily)

### Template 1: Insight add

When someone posts a hot take or observation, add one thing they didn't.

**Format:**
```
Adding to this — [insight that takes the thought further].

[1 short example or data point].
```

**Example use:**

Post: "Win rate is all that matters."
Reply: "Adding to this — but only in 1:1 R:R setups. Asymmetry flips everything. A 40% win rate at 3:1 RR crushes 70% at 0.5:1. Win rate is half the story. R:R asymmetry is the other half."

---

### Template 2: Respectful disagreement

When you genuinely disagree (use sparingly — once a week max per account).

**Format:**
```
Disagree here — [your position in one sentence].

Reason: [the data or experience that backs it up].

But [acknowledge what's right about their take] — that's the part most people miss.
```

**Example use:**

Post: "Trading journals are a waste of time."
Reply: "Disagree here — most journals are a waste of time because they log the wrong things. Broker statement already does that. A useful journal logs your mental state, not your trades. Different question. Most traders never ask the second one."

---

### Template 3: Question that reframes

When someone's framing misses the point.

**Format:**
```
Curious — [question that reframes the problem].

(it matters because [brief reason].)
```

**Example use:**

Post: "How do I stop revenge trading?"
Reply: "Curious — when you revenge trade, do you log it in a journal? (not 'yes/no' — do you log the mental state that led to it? the trigger? what you were feeling 30 min before?) Most traders try to willpower their way out of revenge trading. The fix is logging the lead-up so you can see the pattern before the next one."

---

### Template 4: Personal experience / build-in-public

When someone posts a problem you have direct experience with.

**Format:**
```
Built something for this exact problem, learned [X].

[one sentence about what you learned that adds value to the thread].
```

**Example use:**

Post: "Looking for a trading journal that doesn't take 10 min per trade."
Reply: "Built something for this exact problem, learned that the time-to-log matters more than the depth of the log. My current journal takes 30 sec per trade and catches 90% of the patterns a 10-min log catches. The other 10% isn't worth the time for most traders."

---

### Template 5: Quote-tweet with spin

When you want to amplify a post but add a new angle.

**Format:**
```
Reposting because [angle nobody else has mentioned].
```

**Example use:**

Original post: someone talking about revenge trading
Your quote-tweet: "Reposting because nobody talks about the boring part: revenge trading usually starts 2-3 hours BEFORE the loss. The decision to 'get it back' happens when you see a setup that reminds you of the loss. Logging the lead-up is the only real fix."

## Engagement Rules

- **Never reply with just "great post" or "this is fire."** Always add an insight, a question, or a counter.
- **Never pitch @journalfx in a reply.** Reply with value. If your reply is good, people click your profile and find it. If you pitch, you get muted.
- **Engage with 5-10 accounts/day.** Quality over volume. 3 thoughtful replies beat 10 generic ones.
- **Reply within 2-3 hours of the post when possible.** Twitter rewards early engagement in the algorithm.
- **DM rules:** Only after 2-3 visible public interactions. DM = "hey, i see you journal MT5. built something around that. want to try the beta?" — that's it. No long pitch.
- **Mute fast.** Anyone who replies with hate, scam, or spam — mute immediately. Don't engage with trolls.

---

## Daily 30-Minute Engagement Routine

1. **Open Twitter.** Scroll your "Forex Engagement" list tab. (5 min)
2. **Reply to 3-5 posts from accounts in your list** using templates above. (15 min)
3. **Search "trading journal" / "MT5" / "tilt"** in Twitter. Reply to 1-2 fresh posts from accounts you don't follow yet. (5 min)
4. **Check your notifications** — reply to anyone who replied to you within 24 hours. (5 min)

That's it. 30 min/day, 5 days a week, and you'll have a real presence in the forex Twitter ecosystem within 30 days.

## Weekly 15-Minute Review (do on Sundays)

Answer these 3 questions and write the answers down. Use them to inform week 3-4 content.

1. **Who replied to me this week?** Anyone worth following back / engaging with again?
2. **Which reply of mine got the most engagement?** (Look at your notifications, sort by "Tweets and replies.") What did it have in common with my own posts that worked?
3. **Did anyone DM me about the beta?** If yes — respond within 24 hours. Send them the beta onboarding flow (to be drafted once we have a Tally form set up).
