## What to do after Week 2

**Sunday evening of Week 2: 30-minute retrospective.**

1. **Top 3 posts** of weeks 1-2 (by likes, replies, or quote-tweets). What did they have in common? (Hook type? Length? Topic? Format?)
2. **Bottom 3 posts.** What didn't work? Was it the topic, the time, or the format?
3. **Engagement totals.** Replies, DMs, link clicks, follower count change.
4. **Beta interest.** How many DMs asking about the beta? How many actually converted to testers?
5. **DM-driven asks.** What questions are testers / interested people asking? Anything we should bake into a FAQ?

**Then come back to me with answers to those 5 questions and I'll draft weeks 3-4 based on what actually worked.**

---

## What to do if engagement is near zero after Week 1

Don't panic. Twitter growth is slow at first. The compounding kicks in at week 3-4 if you keep showing up. But if you got literally 0 engagement after 8 posts + 25 replies, three things to check:

1. **Are your replies actually adding value?** Read the 5 reply templates in `engagement-playbook.md` again. Compare yours to the examples. Generic = invisible. Insight = engagement.
2. **Are you engaging with the right accounts?** Tier 1 and Tier 3 in `engagement-playbook.md` are your best bets. Tier 2 takes time to find good ones.
3. **Is your profile telling the right story?** Re-read `bio-and-pin.md`. Does the bio + pinned tweet make a curious trader want to follow?

If all three check out, post week 2 anyway. The compounding takes 3-4 weeks.
