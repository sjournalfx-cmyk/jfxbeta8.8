# Facebook — Weeks 1-2 (4 page posts + group engagement strategy)

Facebook for the SA market is different from Twitter:
- **Facebook Page** (yours, 0 followers) — broadcast mirror of your best content, slightly more polished than Twitter, longer form.
- **Facebook Groups** (SA forex groups, active) — the *real* community channel for SA. Where you add value and get discovered. This is the "community marketing" piece for Facebook.
- **Your personal profile** — use this for group engagement, not the page. People in groups engage with people, not logos.

## Part 2: Facebook Groups — the SA community channel

This is the highest-leverage Facebook move for you. SA forex traders are very active in groups, much more so than on Twitter or Telegram.

### Step 1: Find and join these groups (do this week 1, day 1)

**South African groups (priority):**
- Forex South Africa
- SA Forex Traders
- MT5 Traders South Africa
- Cape Town Forex Traders
- Johannesburg Forex / Joburg Forex
- ZAR USD Traders
- African Forex Traders
- SA Day Traders
- Algo Trading South Africa (or any SA trading-algorithmic group)

**International groups (lower priority but useful):**
- MT5 Traders
- Forex Trading (large, often noisy)
- Trading Psychology
- Trading Journal
- Forex Journaling Community

Search Facebook Groups for these terms. Sort by "Active today" or "Active this week." Join the top 5-10 SA-relevant groups first.

### Step 2: Lurk for 1 week (week 1)

Before posting anything, read what's already in the group:
- What are the rules? (Many groups ban self-promotion outright.)
- What gets engagement? (Trade ideas, journal reviews, "who uses which broker" threads?)
- Who are the active, respected members? (Don't post in groups where the mods are aggressive — your first posts will get nuked.)

### Step 3: Add value for 1-2 weeks (week 2 onward)

After lurking, start adding value. 2-3 group comments per week, using these templates:

**Group reply template 1 (answer a question):**
```
[Direct answer to their question in 1-2 sentences].

What worked for me: [your experience, no JournalFX mention].
```

**Group reply template 2 (share an insight):**
```
Adding to this — [insight that's not the obvious one].

The data I've seen (from my own journal + others): [data point].
```

**Group reply template 3 (helpful resource):**
```
If you want to dig deeper: [link to a free resource — not your app].

The thing that helped me most: [1-sentence insight].
```

### Step 4: After 2-3 weeks of value-add, post "I built this" threads

**Group self-promo post template (use in week 4+ only, and only in groups that allow it):**
```
Built a trading journal for MT5 because none of the existing ones logged the thing that was actually killing my P&L — my head.

Tilt Score, psychological slip detection, MT5 desktop bridge.

Looking for 10-50 traders for a free beta. Happy to share what I'm learning about journaling psychology if anyone wants to discuss.

Not selling anything. Genuinely just want feedback from people who actually journal.

If interested, DM me.
```

**Group self-promo rules:**
- Only post in groups that allow it (check the rules first)
- Only post in 1 group per week (don't spam)
- Don't drop a link — ask for DMs
- Be ready for some pushback. SA forex groups can be skeptical of new tools. Stay humble.

### What to NEVER do in Facebook groups
- Don't drop a JournalFX link in a comment
- Don't DM members who haven't engaged with you publicly first
- Don't post in groups you've just joined (mods will remove it)
- Don't argue with mods — ever

## Part 3: Personal profile vs. page (important)

Use your **personal Facebook profile** for group engagement, not the JournalFX page. Reasons:
- Groups don't let pages post as members
- People engage with people, not brands
- The JournalFX page should be for broadcast only (the 4 posts above)

You can mention in your personal Facebook bio or intro: "Building JournalFX — a trading journal for MT5 traders. Free beta."

That way when group members click your profile, they find the context.

## Part 4: Facebook-specific best practices

- **Posts with photos get 2-3x more reach.** Always include a screenshot or image.
- **First 60 minutes matter.** Reply to every comment on your Page post within 1 hour. Facebook's algorithm rewards early engagement.
- **Native video > YouTube links.** If you ever do video, upload it directly to Facebook.
- **Longer posts work better than on Twitter.** Don't be afraid of 300-500 word posts. Facebook users read.
- **Ask a question at the end of every Page post.** "What mindset tag costs you the most money? Comment below." Simple questions get 5-10x more comments.
- **SA timezone posting.** Best times: 7-9 AM SAST, 12-2 PM SAST, 7-10 PM SAST. Worst: 2-5 AM SAST.
